Stellar Snake Series
A Hierarchical Census of Nearby Stellar Complexes
Tianwen Group
The Stellar Snake series represents a systematic investigation of extended, kinematically coherent stellar complexes in the solar neighbourhood. First discovered in Gaia DR2 (Tian 2020), the Snake has since been revealed as a hierarchically primordial structure spanning hundreds of parsecs, containing thousands of member stars and multiple open clusters. The series explores the formation, dynamics, mass function, chemical evolution, and large-scale census of these snake-like stellar systems — from the original 30–40 Myr structure to a full 3 kpc census of over 1,100 stellar snake candidates.

Molecular cloud density increases with Galactic longitude. Older clusters form in cavities near high-density regions, while young field stars form in present-day high-density environments — revealing the role of cloud density in regulating star-formation sequences.

Paper IV

2026 ApJL, 999, L24
Emergence of a Lithium Dip in ∼35 Myr “Snake” Open Clusters
Zhang, Y.-Y., Tian, H.-J.*, Shi, J.-R., Xie, C.-C., Yang, X.-M.
Discovery of a lithium dip (Li dip) in the 35±5 Myr Snake, challenging the classical view that Li dips emerge only at ages ≳150 Myr. Fast rotators exhibit stronger lithium depletion than slow rotators.

Rotation–Lithium Depletion Correlation
Zhang, Y.-Y., Tian, H.-J.*, Shi, J.-R., Xie, C.-C., Yang, X.-M.
Within the dip temperature range (6200–6800 K), a significant correlation is found between rotational velocity and lithium depletion. Fast rotators (v sin i > 25 km s⁻¹) exhibit stronger Li depletion, suggesting rotational shear enhances turbulent mixing and accelerates Li destruction.

Paper II

2024 MNRAS, 530, 4970
The Stellar “Snake” – II. The Mass Function
Yang, X.-M., Bird, S.A., Li, J., Tian, H.-J.*, Qiu, D., et al.
A comprehensive investigation of the mass function (MF) of the Snake. Systematic variations in the MF along the elongation direction are discovered: the “head” follows a canonical IMF (α ∼ −2.3), while the “tail” becomes more top-light, indicating delayed formation of massive stars.

Binary Fraction & Mass-Ratio Distribution

Using a simulated CMD-based inference method, the binary fraction (q > 0.3) for Snake clusters is concentrated between 30% and 50%, with predominantly negative γ, indicating lower-mass companions in binary systems.

Paper I

2022 MNRAS, 513, 503
The Stellar “Snake” – I. Whole Structure and Properties
Wang, F., Tian, H.-J.*, Qiu, D., Xu, Q., Fang, M., et al.
The first comprehensive census of the Snake, revealing a hierarchically primordial structure with over 10,000 member stars, 11 open clusters, and an age of 30–40 Myr. The structure is expanding and best explained as formed from a filamentary giant molecular cloud.

2022 MNRAS, 513, 503
3D Expansion Rates & Orbital Analysis
Wang, F., Tian, H.-J.*, Qiu, D., Xu, Q., Fang, M., et al.
The Snake is divided into four kinematic groups. Expansion ages (∼33 Myr) are consistent with the stellar age. Asymmetric expansion and a significant contraction signature in the Z-direction for Group III reveal complex dynamical evolution.

Initial Discovery

2020 ApJ, 904, 196
Discovery of a Young Stellar Snake with Two Dissolving Cores in the Solar Neighborhood
Tian, H.-J.*
The original discovery of the stellar Snake — a young (30–40 Myr) snake-like structure in the solar neighbourhood from Gaia DR2. Spanning >200 pc with two dissolving cores, the Snake is hierarchically primordial and likely extends the Vela OB2 association.
View on IOPscience
2020 ApJ, 904, 196
Two Dissolving Cores & Mass Function
Tian, H.-J.*
The Snake contains two dissolving cores (NGC 2232 and Tian 2) clearly distinguished in 6D phase space. Total mass >2000 M⊙. The structure is too young to be explained by tidal tails, supporting a hierarchical primordial formation scenario.
